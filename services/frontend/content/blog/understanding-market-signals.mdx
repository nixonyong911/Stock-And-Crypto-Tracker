## Understanding Market Signals

When you receive a market signal, it's natural to wonder: what does this actually mean for me? This guide will help you understand how to interpret and use the insights you receive.

## What Is a Market Signal?

A market signal is an indication that something noteworthy is happening with a particular asset. It could be:

- A technical pattern forming on the chart
- Unusual volume activity
- Price approaching key support or resistance levels
- Momentum shifts in either direction

## Reading Your Signals

### Signal Type

Every signal we send includes its type. Common types include:

- **Breakout** - Price moving above resistance or below support
- **Reversal** - Potential change in trend direction
- **Continuation** - Trend likely to continue
- **Consolidation** - Price moving sideways

### Time Horizon

We indicate whether the signal is relevant for:

- Short-term (hours to days)
- Medium-term (days to weeks)
- Long-term (weeks to months)

### Confidence Level

Our AI provides a confidence assessment:

- **High** - Strong technical evidence
- **Medium** - Moderate technical evidence
- **Low** - Early or weak signals

## What Signals Are NOT

Important reminders:

- Signals are not financial advice
- They do not guarantee future performance
- They should be part of your broader analysis
- Always apply your own risk management

## Using Signals Wisely

The best approach to using market signals:

1. Treat them as one data point among many
2. Always do your own research
3. Never invest more than you can afford to lose
4. Consider your own risk tolerance
5. Consult a financial advisor for personalized advice

Stay disciplined, stay informed, and happy trading!
